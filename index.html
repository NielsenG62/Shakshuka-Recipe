Shakshuka

Recipe from: cooking.nytimes.com

Servings: 4-6

Cook Time: 50 Minutes

Shakshuka (pronounced Shahk-Shoo-Kah) is a traditional Mediterranean dish, made up of eggs, tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, and garlic. Traditionally, this vegetarian dish is served for breakfast, but this is one of those meals that can fit into any time of day. This is an easy, one-dish meal that has loads of flavor, and is very easy to customize to your particular tastes. Good sides include bread (pita or challah works best) or a salad.

Tags: Vegetarian, Eggs, Mediterranean, One-Dish

Ingredients

  • 3
    Tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1
    Large onion, halved and thinly sliced
  • 1
    Large red bell pepper, seeded and thinly sliced
  • 3
    Garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1
    Teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1
    Teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1/8
    Teaspoon ground cayenne, or to taste
  • 1
    (28-ounce) can whole plum tomatoes with their juices, coarsely chopped
  • 3/4
    Teaspoon kosher salt, plus more as needed
  • 1/4
    Teaspoon black pepper, plus more as needed
  • 5
    Ounces feta, crumbled (about 1 1/4 cups)
  • 6
    Large eggs
  • Chopped cilantro, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Heat skillet with oil on medium-low . Add onion and bell pepper, and cook about 20 minutes until soft.
  3. Stir in garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add tomatoes, salt, and pepper, simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in feta.
  6. Crack eggs onto dish and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Transfer to oven until eggs are set, 7 to 10 minutes.
  8. Sprinkle with cilantro.
